Introduction back to ToC

VSSo is a Vehicle Signal and Attribute ontology. Is is meant to be used as a domain ontology for [SOSA] or the [Web of Things]. Signals are SOSA Observable or Actuatable properties, while static attributes are properties of Vehicle branches. The units used are declared in [QUDT] unit types, and do not restrict to a specific unit (e.g. qudt:LinearVelocityUnit enables the usage of km/h or mph). Likewise, Attributes that refer to a phenonmenon requiring a unit are restricted to [custom-datatypes] refering to the correct unit system (e.g. vsso:maxPower is in custom-datatype: power) In VSSo, signals are functions that convey "information about the behavior or attributes of some phenomenon" [Signal]. As such, they may or may not relate to sensors or actuators, and always to the phenomenon they describe. We often refer to a signal by the phenomenon it is related to. For example, an instance of vsso:AmbientAirTemperature is a signal referring to the temperature measured by some sensor of a vehicle, and is readable.
